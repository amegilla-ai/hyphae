# Capacity

How much social energy you have available right now.

## What it captures

Your current bandwidth for relationship work - reaching out, showing up, holding space for others. Finite and variable. Not the same as free time or calendar space; capacity is the *energy* you have for social interaction, which can be low even on an empty weekend.

## When to fill it in

At each check-in. Capacity is a weekly-ish signal, not a permanent setting - it changes as life changes.

## Values

### low

You are running close to empty. Showing up for people feels costly right now. Prioritise rest and existing commitments over new reach-outs.

### medium

Steady baseline. You have energy for the people in your close layers and a modest amount for others.

### high

You have energy to spare. A good time to reach further - reconnect with someone you've gone quiet with, deepen a relationship you've been wanting to invest in, check in on people beyond your usual rhythm.

## Why it exists

Neurodiverse adults often experience variable social energy that looks nothing like neurotypical patterns. A system that nudges at the same rate regardless of capacity would push the user into overwhelm during low-capacity periods and under-surface opportunities during high-capacity ones. Capacity lets the system follow the user's actual state.

## Agent guidance

Read the most recent check-in's capacity to scale nudge volume for this week. Low capacity = fewer, lower-commitment suggestions, minimum-floor maintenance only. Medium = layer default. High = room to surface reconnect or deepen goals the user hasn't had bandwidth for. Never pile on when capacity is low; never under-surface when it is high.

**Soften, don't suspend.** During low capacity, keep noticing long silences but soften the response - suggest the lowest-commitment mode (a voice note, a brief text), frame as "if you want", never as obligation. Don't pile up a backlog of missed nudges to hit the user with when capacity returns. For extended low-capacity periods (weeks+), research on autistic burnout (Raymaker 2020, Mantzalas 2022) shows full social withdrawal compounds the problem - mention any concerning lapse once gently, not weekly, and frame it as availability. See `docs/nd-dunbar-research.md` finding 4.
